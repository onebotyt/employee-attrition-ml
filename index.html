💡 Why Recall is the primary metric: Missing an employee who is about to leave (false negative) means losing their institutional knowledge, paying recruitment and training costs (avg. 50–200% of annual salary). It costs far more to miss an at-risk employee than to offer unnecessary retention incentives.

Key Finding
Top Attrition Drivers
Identified by Random Forest feature importance — actionable for HR strategy.
1
OverTime
Overtime employees leave at 3× the base rate — reduce mandatory overtime
2
MonthlyIncome
Low-income employees (<$3K) have highest attrition — salary review needed
3
YearsAtCompany
New employees (<3 years) are highest risk — improve onboarding & mentoring
4
JobSatisfaction
Score 1 employees leave far more — regular engagement surveys + action
5
WorkLifeBalance
Poor WLB strongly predicts attrition — flexible work arrangements help
